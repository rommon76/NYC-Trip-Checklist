NYC-specific tips

  • Tipping is expected, not optional. 18–20% at sit-down restaurants (some now add it automatically — check the bill), $1–2 per drink at bars, $1–2 per bag for hotel porters, 15–20% for taxis/Uber, $2–5 for housekeeping per night left daily.
  • Transit — use OMNY, not MetroCard. Just tap your contactless credit/debit card or phone (Apple Pay / Google Pay) at the turnstile. Fare capping kicks in after 12 rides in a week (effectively free unlimited).
  • Safety: NYC is very safe in tourist areas but stay aware — don't leave bags hanging on chair backs, be cautious on late-night empty subway platforms, use the front carriages near the conductor after dark.
  • Jaywalking is a way of life but cars turn on green lights too — always check.
  • Sales tax (~8.875%) is added at the register, not shown on price tags. Factor this in.
  • Tap water is excellent and free — refill your bottle everywhere.
  • Sunday Broadway shows and Monday museum closures — double-check your itinerary.
  • Time difference: NYC is 14 hours behind Sydney (during NYC's daylight saving). Plan calls home accordingly.

NYC weather · early-to-mid May (your dates)

  • Typical daytime highs: 18–23°C (64–74°F). Sunny days can push to 25°C+.
  • Overnight lows: 10–13°C (50–55°F) — evenings feel noticeably cool.
  • Rain: May averages ~10 rainy days/month. Expect at least 2–3 showers across 13 nights. Pack a compact umbrella and a packable rain layer.
  • Layering is essential. Mornings cool, middays warm, evenings chilly again. Indoors (subway, restaurants) is heavily air-conditioned.
  • UV: Moderate — sunscreen still worth packing, especially for rooftop/observation-deck days.
  • Tip: check a 10-day forecast 3 days before you fly and adjust — May can swing.
